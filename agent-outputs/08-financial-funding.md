### Strategic Implications

**For a Potential Investor:**

1. **Due diligence priority is verifying claimed metrics.** The gap between stated scale and zero external validation is the single biggest concern. Request Google Analytics / Similarweb verification, listing database access, and user cohort data before any further analysis.

2. **The global-first approach is high-risk, high-reward.** If Criby has genuinely built a platform serving 100+ countries from scratch in ~1-2 years, the execution velocity is remarkable and worth funding. But this claim contradicts the typical capital requirements of comparable companies.

3. **Revenue model clarity is essential.** The website shows no visible monetization (no premium listings, no agent tools, no subscription tiers). Understanding how they plan to generate revenue is critical before any investment thesis can be formed.

4. **If metrics are accurate, they should be raising NOW.** The proptech funding environment in 2025-2026 is the most favorable since 2021. A company with genuine 1M+ MAU across 100+ countries would attract significant interest from Fifth Wall, MetaProp, and others. The fact that they haven't raised publicly suggests either (a) they don't want to, (b) they can't validate the metrics, or (c) they're preparing for a stealth raise.

5. **Aggregation-based marketplace models face defensibility challenges.** If Criby is aggregating listings from other portals (like Nestpick did from 200+ partners), the moat is thin. Deep local partnerships, proprietary data, and user lock-in would need to be demonstrated.

**For a Competitor Monitoring This:**

1. The global aggregation play has been attempted before (Nestpick, acquired for undisclosed terms likely below its $13M raised). It has not yet produced a breakout winner.
2. If Criby's tech stack genuinely supports 70+ languages and 100+ countries with a small team, the technology (likely AI-driven translation and listing aggregation) could be the real asset, not the marketplace itself.
